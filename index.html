Day 2 · Sun 5 Apr
🏛️ Westminster + Covent Garden
9 activities
›
09:30
Tube to Westminster
District or Circle line
09:45
Big Ben
View from Westminster Bridge. The iconic 96-metre Elizabeth Tower has been keeping time since 1859. Best photos from the middle of Westminster Bridge looking north. Free to admire from outside.
10:15
London Eye Walk
Stroll along the South Bank. Europe's tallest cantilevered observation wheel at 135m. A ride takes 30 min and tickets cost ~£32. Even if you skip the ride, the South Bank walk has stunning Parliament views.
10:45
Westminster Abbey
Gothic masterpiece where every British monarch has been crowned since 1066. Entry is ~£27 if you want to go inside. The exterior and grounds are stunning for photos even without a ticket.
11:15
St James's Park & Buckingham Palace
London's oldest Royal Park with pelicans and lake views. Walk through to reach Buckingham Palace — the Changing of the Guard happens at 11:00 on select days (check the schedule). Free to view from the gates.
12:45
Lunch · Dishoom Covent Garden
12 Upper St Martin's Lane. Bombay-inspired cafe beloved by Londoners — try the black daal and bacon naan roll. Expect queues at peak times; your booking avoids the wait. Mains around £12–18.
✓ BOOKED
14:15
Covent Garden Area
Piazza · Apple Market · Neal's Yard · Seven Dials. Free street performers in the Piazza, artisan stalls in the Apple Market, and the Instagram-famous colourful courtyard of Neal's Yard. Allow 1–2 hours to explore.
18:45
Dinner · Hawksmoor Seven Dials
11 Langley St, Covent Garden. Widely considered London's best steakhouse — the 35-day dry-aged ribeye is legendary. Expect to spend £50–70pp with drinks. The pre-theatre menu (until 18:30) is great value at ~£30.
✓ BOOKED
21:00
Optional: Soho Drinks
Explore Old Compton St or Carnaby area. Soho is London's buzzing nightlife hub — Old Compton St is the heart of the scene, packed with bars and restaurants. Carnaby St nearby has quirky independent shops and cocktail bars.
Day 3 · Mon 6 Apr
🔭 Science + Notting Hill
4 activities
›
10:00
Natural History Museum or Science Museum
Exhibition Road, South Kensington. Both are completely free! The Natural History Museum has the famous dinosaur gallery and stunning Romanesque architecture. The Science Museum has hands-on tech exhibits. Open 10:00–17:50.
13:15
Lunch · Muriel's Kitchen
36 Old Brompton Rd, South Kensington. Charming brunch-style cafe with a cosy interior. Known for their pancakes, eggs royale, and afternoon tea. Mains around £12–16, no reservation needed.
14:30
Portobello Road Market
Lancaster Road colourful houses. World-famous antique and street food market stretching over a mile through Notting Hill. Note: visiting on Monday means the antique stalls will be closed (Saturday is the main day), but the permanent shops, cafes and colourful houses along Lancaster Road are still worth the visit.
19:00
Dinner · The Ladbroke Arms
54 Ladbroke Rd, Notting Hill. Award-winning gastropub with an upscale British menu — think slow-cooked lamb and seasonal fish. Cosy interior with a proper pub feel. Mains £18–28, booking recommended.
✓ BOOKED
Day 4 · Tue 7 Apr
🏰 Tower + Borough + Skyline
7 activities
›
09:00
Tower of London · Crown Jewels
Tower Hill, EC3N 4AB. Nearly 1,000-year-old fortress housing the Crown Jewels, the famous ravens, and centuries of royal history. Tickets ~£33, open 09:00–17:30. Head to the Jewel House first to beat the crowds.
✓ BOOKED
11:15
Tower Bridge
Walk across + views of the Thames. London's most famous bridge with its iconic Victorian Gothic towers. Walking across is free. The Glass Floor Exhibition up top costs ~£12 and offers a thrilling see-through view down to the river.
12:30
Lunch · Borough Market
8 Southwark St, SE1 1TL. London's oldest food market (1,000+ years!). Packed with artisan stalls — try the raclette, scotch eggs, or padella pasta. Open Wed–Sat. Come hungry and graze from stall to stall, ~£10–15 for a great meal.
13:45
Thames Path Walk
Millennium Bridge · St Paul's Cathedral view. The pedestrian-only steel suspension bridge offers one of London's best views — St Paul's dome framed perfectly at the end. Completely free, and a great photo spot.
16:30
Rest at Hotel
Freshen up before evening
19:30
Sky Garden
1 Sky Garden Walk, EC3M 8AF. London's highest public garden on floors 35–37 of the "Walkie Talkie" building. Entry is free but you must book a slot in advance. 360° panoramic views of the city skyline, plus bars and restaurants.
✓ BOOKED
21:00
Dinner · Bleecker Burger
Bloomberg Arcade, City of London. Cult-favourite smash burgers — their dry-aged beef patties are simple and perfect. Burgers £9–13, no booking needed. The Bloomberg Arcade location is sleek and usually less crowded than others.
Day 5 · Wed 8 Apr
🚂 King's Cross + Cabaret
4 activities
›
10:00
King's Cross Platform 9¾
King's Cross Station. The Harry Potter photo op with a trolley disappearing into the wall — there's a dedicated shop next to it. Free to take photos but expect a 15–30 min queue. Coal Drops Yard nearby has stylish shops and cafes in a converted Victorian coal store.
13:00
Lunch · Circolo Popolare
40-41 Rathbone Pl, Fitzrovia W1T. Stunning Italian restaurant with walls covered in thousands of bottles — one of London's most Instagrammed interiors. Big sharing plates of pasta and pizza, mains £14–22. Booking essential.
✓ BOOKED
14:45
Regent's Park or Soho
Afternoon leisure — your pick. Regent's Park is 395 acres of gorgeous gardens, a boating lake, and London Zoo. Free entry, open from 05:00 to dusk. Or head to Soho for vintage shopping, record stores, and people-watching.
19:00
The London Cabaret Club
Dinner show at the Bloomsbury Ballroom. A glamorous evening of live cabaret, burlesque, and a 3-course dinner in a stunning 1930s Art Deco ballroom. Smart dress code — no trainers or sportswear. Show starts at 19:00.
✓ BOOKED
